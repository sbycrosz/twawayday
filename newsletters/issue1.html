ONLY 5 WEEKS TO GO!
 

To whet your appetite for what is going to be an amazing weekend, we’ve put together some ideas for things to do around our Away Day venue, Changi Cove.


LOCATION

Changi Cove is located at 351 Cranwell Road, Singapore.

 
WHAT TO DO AROUND HERE

CHANGI BOARDWALK
2.2km long. It takes about 45 mins to complete the entire walk. Open 24 hours and admission is free.

CHANGI VILLAGE

Variety of eateries, from welcoming modern cafes to hawker centers serving local delights. Fishing equipment stores, convenience stores and bicycle rental services are also to be found at Changi Village.


PULAU UBIN (MEANS TILE ISLAND)

It is an island located off the North Eastern shore of Singapore. Take a $2.50 bumboat ride from the Changi Ferry Terminal. (Trekking. Bicycle rental $8 approx). There is a seafood restaurant on the island, and you may spot wild boars there.


CHEK JAWA WETLANDS

It is a nature preservation of rare plants, local and migratory birds and natural creatures such as fiddler crabs and monitor lizards are home to one of Singapore’s richest ecosystems. Opening hours are 08:30am to 6:00pm.

 

CHANGI CHAPEL MUSEUM

The museum preserves a picture of the life of the prisoners of war during the Japanese Occupation.


INFO FOR INTERNATIONAL GUESTS


SINGAPORE

 

While Singapore’s name derives from the Malay words for “lion” and “city”, studies suggest that lions have never lived here. The Malay prince Sang Nila Utama is said to have founded Singapore in the 13th century and given it the name Singapura after seeing an impressive wild animal on the island. In all likelihood, the animal was a Malayan Tiger.
